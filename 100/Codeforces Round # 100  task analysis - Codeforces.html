Codeforces Round # 100: task analysis
Author Natalia , 3 days ago ,

Problem A. New Year's table

Plates should touch the edge of the table, and therefore, their centers must lie on a circle of radius R - r (see figure). 

If the plates have the largest possible radius of the table, their centers will be located at the vertices of a regular n-gon inscribed in this circle. The problem boils down to, to find the length of the inscribed regular n-gon, and compare it with 2r. The formula for the length of  a  = 2 ( R  -  R ) sin (p ​​/  n ) is easily deduced. To do this, consider a right triangle, drawn in green.

When implemented carefully need to allocate the case when the equation R  = ( R  -  R ) sin (p ​​/  n ) (*). Because of the computational error the right side can get more left, which will lead to the answer "NO" instead of "YES". Comparison in such cases should be conducted with the participation of small e : 
a  + e <  b instead of a  <  b ,
a  <  b  + e instead of a  ≤  b .
The constant ε must be chosen so that it was less than the possible difference between the exact values ​​of a and b , if they are not equal. In particular, in calculations by the formula (*) under the constraints of the task, this difference may amount to about 7 × 10  - 7 . Therefore, ε = 10  - 7 is sufficient, and ε = 10  - 6 - no longer exists. 

Once again, bring a focus on the fact that the choice of ε  depends on the specific formula, which is evaluated and compared. Different solutions may be held and not held to the same e  .

The first column of the table contains a set of cards, which are obtained from Alexander after receipt of each regular cards. Numbers in the set are recorded in order of priority of Alexander. Each i-th of the following four columns contains the number of cards that will get the i-th one of the appropriate sets. Our goal - to choose for each other's most preferred card of his column. 

Note that to the current set of Alexander, and another number to determine which card will go to this friend, do not necessarily know the whole set of Alexander. We only have two top priorities of cards. Therefore, for each time we find two highest priority among the available cards. Using them to determine which of them will go to each other (count columns in the table). Then choose an element with the highest priority in each column. Obtain the solution for O ( n 2 ) .

Problem D. New Year's Contest

Solution 1 (greed). The optimal procedure for resolving the problems - in ascending order (non-decreasing) complexity . At the same problems solved before the new year, you need to send at 0:00, resolved after the new year - just after their decision.

Why such a procedure is optimal, we prove the method of descent . The general line of reasoning is this: suppose you have the best answer, in which tasks are not in order of increasing complexity. We show that it can be to go ("go down") to another option, which is not less than optimal. As a result, such steps we arrive at a sorted version.

Thus, we assume that the optimal response to a couple of problems, difficulties which are, in descending order. Then there are two problems to be solved in a row and have that property. Suppose they both settled before the New Year. Then by changing the order of their total penalty time will not change (and therefore does not deteriorate). If both problems are solved after the new year, their contribution to the total penalty time ( T  +  a I ) + ( T  +  a I  +  a j ​​) , where T - time of the beginning of the first of these solutions (numbered I), a I - while solving the first problem, a j  <  a I - while solving the second problem. The interchange of these two problems get penalty time ( T  +  a j ​​) + ( T  +  a j  ​​+  a I ) , which is less than  ( T  +  a I ) + ( T  +  a I  +  a j ​​) . It remains to consider cases where one of the adjacent tasks in the "wrong" order, "crosses the New Year." These cases are treated similarly.

If Gennadi does not have time to solve every problem in the allotted time, you should choose the largest possible number of the most simple tasks. Indeed, it makes no sense to solve a complex problem and not solve the simpler: the replacement of more a I will not worsen to a smaller response. Rigorous analysis can be made ​​by the same method of descent.

Solution 2 (dynamics). First, as in the previous solution, we choose the most easiest of tasks that have time to decide Gennady within the allotted time. Discard the remaining tasks. Will move the problem to be solved directly in the New Year (0:00). Other tasks, but her need to be divided into two sets, one of which is decided before the New Year, the other - after it. Tasks in the second set must be solved in order of increasing complexity (well-known fact for those who participate in competitions under the rules ACM). In the first set of solutions order is irrelevant. Sort given numbers in ascending order and calculate the dynamics of the d [i] [j] [k] - the smallest penalty that can be obtained by solving the first i tasks are j - after the New Year, with the latter problem is solved after the new year at the time k. Note that by (i, j, k) is uniquely determined by the number of problems solved before the new year, and the total time to solve them. After calculating the dynamics of recall about the problem which was solved by direct at 0:00 (and which was not involved in the calculation of dynamics). Loop time before the new year when it started its decision and take into account other problems with the dynamics counted.
Task E. Christmas garland

First, learn how to solve the subproblem for one tier: to make a garland of length s of the bulbs exactly k colors so that no two bulbs of one color were not in a row. At the same options differ in the order of colors, while the same view (if necessary, we always have time to multiply k!). Subproblem solution, we need only k  ≤  s  ≤ 5000 , so consider the dynamics of O ( s 2 ) :
a [s] [k] = a [s-1] [k-1] + a [s-1] [k] * (k -1).
This would be the Stirling numbers of the second kind , if not the condition that the same bulb can not go straight.  

Next, calculate the dynamics of the d [i] [j] - number of ways to make a garland for the first i layers (including rules) to the i-th layer was exactly j different colors. States will be of the order of L (total length of the garland), because at every tier of the different colors is not more than the length of the stage: j  ≤  L I (!). Transitions can also be done in O ( L ) , given the fact that many flowers of different power different (!!). Indeed, let d [I] [j] = A m j  * a [L [I]] [j] * (sum of all d [i-1] [k]), and then subtract the options that set the i th and (i-1) th layers of the same are obtained. The number of placements of A m j  =  m ( m  - 1) ... ( m  -  j  + 1) can be predposchitat, so they are only for j up to 5000. 

Thus, the author's solution works for the O ( L  +  s 2 ) ( L  ≤ 10 7 , s  ≤ 5000 ), and does not use the definition (only the operations of addition and multiplication by a given module).
Task F. Christmas snowflake

To begin with, how to check a candidate for the center of symmetry ( x C ,  y C ) . Consider all the points are symmetric with respect to a ( x I ,  y I ) . They have the form (2 x C  -  x I , 2 y C  -  y I ) . Need to find out whether it was true that all but at most k points contained in the set {( x I ,  y I )} . This can be done for the binary search (pre-sorting the given set). However, there is a quick way to check for O ( n ) . Note that if the initial point ( x I ,  y I ) have been streamlined, for example, by increasing x, and with equal x - ascending y, the points of the form (2 x C  -   x I , 2 y C  -  y I ) are obtained in the reverse order of sort. Regardless of the center ( x C ,  y C ) . Therefore, if the check point  (2 x C  -   x I , 2 y C  -  y I ) in order, you can simply move the cursor to the end of a sorted array ( x I ,  y I ) .

From the preceding arguments that if the set has a center of symmetry, for the first point (in the sort order), the n-th pair, the second - (n-1)-I, etc. Given that k points can not be paired, cross first (k +1) point to the beginning of the array, and (k +1) from the end. For each pair of points we find the middle of the segment connecting them, and execute its test in O ( n ) . Asymptotics of the solution - O ( NK ​​2 ) .
